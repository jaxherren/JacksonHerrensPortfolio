About
Jackson

I first found my interest in fashion through the skate and street culture I grew up surrounded by. As I got older, that fascination matured into a deeper appreciation for designers, archival garments, and the small details that make certain pieces stand out — and from there I became more and more interested in learning how those garments were actually made.

I'm currently studying Apparel Merchandising & Management at Cal Poly Pomona, where I've built skills in garment analysis, textiles, Adobe Illustrator, sewing, trend research, and marketing. Outside of school, my work at Etelier Gallery has pushed me into product development, creative direction, brand storytelling, and campaign development — applying what I've learned in a real, tangible way.

Many of my interests outside of fashion — vintage cars, photography, music, film — continue to influence how I approach creative work. I think it's important to have passions that live both inside and outside your career. They give you perspective, and they make the work more personal.
